The natural-language processing group at Harvard (harvardnlp) is a collection of researchers and students (both graduate and undergraduate) interested in computational methods for human language. This includes machine learning methodology, tasks requiring artificial intelligence, and cognitive understanding of language. Recently we have been following developments in deep learning for natural language processing.

If you are interested this area, we suggest taking one of the courses we offer in the area. We also hold a weekly reading group where we discuss developments in the field and invite local speakers.

Our research group also publishes research on machine learning for natural-language processing among other topics. The recent focus of our work has been on character-based language modeling, deep learning for coreference resolution, and automatic summarization of sentences. We also aim to release code to the research community.
